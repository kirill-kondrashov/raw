# Draft: MLC research program

## Overall idea

Let 

- $\mathcal{M}$ - Mandelbrot set,
- $\mathcal{S}$ - set of classes of subsets of $\mathcal{M}$, i.e. given $m_1, m_2 \subset \mathcal{M}$ subset of $\mathcal{M}$, equivalence relation $m_1 \sim_{comp} m_2$ means that they lie in one class $\left[ m_1 \right] = \left[ m_2 \right] \in \mathcal{S}$.

Observe that
- the equivalnce relation $\sim_{comp}$ can be defined in many ways,
- one of the reasonable ways is to suppose that each subset $m \subset \mathcal{M}$ can be approximated by $m^\prime$ s. t. $m \in m^\prime$ and $m^\prime$ is a polyhedra with insides, homotopically equivalent to a disk $D^2$, two sets $m_1$ and $m_2$ are equivalent if they can be approximated by same $m^\prime$. Approximated here means that given $\varepsilon > 0$, $|m^\prime \Delta m| < \varepsilon$.
